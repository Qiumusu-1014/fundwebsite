UAE Opens 2026 With Robust Economic Indicators Across Banking, Trade and Finance

ABU DHABI, UAE - The UAE economy maintained growth momentum at the start of 2026, with banking assets, credit, deposits and foreign-trade data all pointing to strong resilience.

According to official data and reports cited by the Emirates News Agency, total UAE banking assets rose 1.1% month on month to more than AED 5.472 trillion by February 2026. Total credit increased 1.2% to AED 2.63 trillion, while bank deposits grew 1.9% to AED 3.4 trillion.

In early March, the UAE banking sector recorded a capital adequacy ratio of 17% and a liquidity coverage ratio above 146.6%, continuing to exceed international regulatory standards.

On sovereign credit, Moody's maintained the UAE's Aa2 rating with a stable outlook, while S&P Global Ratings affirmed its foreign and local currency sovereign ratings at AA/A-1+ with a stable outlook. S&P noted that the UAE economy is supported by strong fiscal and economic resilience, as well as high levels of government net assets and liquid assets.

Trade indicators also remained strong. In 2025, the UAE's total foreign trade reached AED 6 trillion, up 15% year on year. Services trade exceeded AED 1.14 trillion for the first time, while non-oil goods trade rose 27% to AED 3.8 trillion. The UAE also entered the world's top 10 merchandise-exporting economies for the first time, and Dubai rose to seventh place in the Global Financial Centres Index.

News Watch: Financial stability, trade expansion and international ratings reinforce the UAE's competitiveness as a regional center for capital, trade and corporate headquarters.

Sources: WAM, Moody's, S&P Global Ratings
