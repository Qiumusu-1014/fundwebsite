UAE Announces Exit From OPEC and OPEC+, Citing National Energy Strategy

ABU DHABI, UAE - The United Arab Emirates announced that it will withdraw from the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC) and OPEC+ effective May 1, 2026, saying the decision follows a comprehensive review of national production capacity, production policy and long-term economic strategy.

According to the Emirates News Agency, the UAE stressed that leaving the mechanisms does not mean stepping away from its responsibility to support global energy-market stability. Instead, the move is intended to give the country greater policy flexibility in a changing energy landscape and allow it to respond more directly to shifts in supply and demand.

The UAE said it will continue to participate in global energy markets in a responsible, reliable and forward-looking manner, gradually and orderly bringing additional production capacity to market according to prevailing conditions.

The adjustment marks a major turning point in UAE energy policy. It reflects sustained investment across the energy value chain, including oil and gas, renewable energy and low-carbon technologies, as well as a renewed focus on domestic capacity and the needs of investors, customers and partners.

International markets are watching the decision closely amid continued pressure on regional supply chains and maritime routes. Reuters reported that OPEC+ later agreed to a modest quota increase for June, but the increment no longer included the UAE share after its May 1 exit, while restrictions linked to the Strait of Hormuz continued to limit the real impact of added output.

News Watch: The decision may reshape the UAE's role in global energy governance, giving it wider room to balance traditional oil and gas, low-carbon transition and energy security.

Sources: WAM, Reuters
