Abu Dhabi Hosts Historic "Global South" Economic Forum

ABU DHABI, UAE — Royals, Heads of State, and business leaders from over 50 nations gathered in Abu Dhabi yesterday for the inaugural "Global South Economic Forum."

The forum focused on creating equitable trade partnerships and sustainable development financing for emerging markets. During the plenary session, UAE Royal Capital Funding announced a major pledge of $1 billion specifically targeted at sustainable agriculture and water infrastructure projects in Africa and South Asia.

"Prosperity must be shared to be sustainable," stated Sheikh Mohammed bin Hamdan Al Nahyan. "This fund is not aid; it is an investment in the future food security of the world."

The initiative has drawn praise from the UN and the World Bank, positioning the UAE as a compassionate and strategic leader in global development.
