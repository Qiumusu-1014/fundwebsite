Emirates Advances Closed-Loop Recycling Programme With 88,000 kg of Plastic Reused

Emirates has repurposed more than 88,000 kilograms of inflight plastic meal-service items through a Dubai-based closed-loop recycling programme supported by an AED 50 million investment.

Emirates aviation sustainability circular economy recycling Dubai deSter inflight dining

DUBAI, UAE - Emirates announced on June 4, 2026, that it has repurposed more than 88,000 kilograms of plastic from Economy Class inflight meal-service items over the past year, turning used onboard materials into new products for future flights.

The airline said the initiative forms part of a closed-loop recycling programme first launched in June 2023. Damaged and unserviceable trays, casseroles, snack dishes and bowls are collected after flights, cleaned, inspected and processed at a specialist facility in Dubai before being transformed into new meal-service items.

According to Emirates, the new items can contain up to 25% recycled material and are returned to Emirates Flight Catering for reintroduction across thousands of flights. The programme is supported by more than AED 50 million invested in the transition to a closed-loop manufacturing model for inflight dining serviceware.

The programme is delivered in partnership with deSter FZE UAE, an aviation serviceware provider with expertise in closed-loop manufacturing. Emirates said the facility also applies sustainable design principles, including solar power, efficient water management and waste-minimisation practices.

For investors and business observers, the project highlights Dubai's role in linking aviation, local manufacturing and circular-economy execution. By processing materials locally and returning them into the airline supply chain, the model supports waste reduction while strengthening regional capabilities around sustainable aviation services.
