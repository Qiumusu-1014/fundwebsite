Hamdan bin Mohammed Approves AED 1 Billion Economic Incentives for Dubai

DUBAI, UAE - Sheikh Hamdan bin Mohammed approved AED 1 billion in temporary economic incentives designed to support businesses and reinforce Dubai's economic momentum.

The package was positioned as a measure to ease pressure on companies, support liquidity and sustain private-sector confidence across priority industries.

The decision aligns with the Dubai Economic Agenda D33, which seeks to expand the emirate's economy, deepen global trade links and strengthen Dubai's appeal as a destination for headquarters, entrepreneurs and investors.

News Watch: The incentive package is important because it combines near-term business relief with a long-term competitiveness agenda, helping Dubai maintain confidence during periods of global uncertainty.
