Dubai Chamber of Commerce Organises 674 B2B Meetings to Strengthen Dubai-South Africa Trade

Dubai Chamber of Commerce coordinated 674 bilateral business meetings in Johannesburg and Cape Town during a South Africa trade mission featuring 24 Dubai-based companies.

Dubai Chamber South Africa trade investment B2B meetings New Horizons non-oil trade Africa Johannesburg Cape Town

DUBAI, UAE - Dubai Chamber of Commerce announced on June 5, 2026, that it organised 674 bilateral business meetings between Dubai-based companies and counterparts in Johannesburg and Cape Town during a trade mission to South Africa.

The mission was carried out under the chamber's New Horizons initiative, which is designed to help local companies expand into promising global markets and build international partnerships. The delegation included representatives from 24 Dubai-based companies across construction and building materials, electronics, engineering, environmental solutions, fast-moving consumer goods, food and beverages, healthcare, ICT and innovation, investments, manufacturing and retail.

As part of the mission, Dubai Chamber of Commerce also organised the Dubai-South Africa Business Forum in cooperation with the UAE Embassy in Pretoria, South Africa's Department of Trade, Industry and Competition, the South African Chamber of Commerce and Industry and the Johannesburg Chamber of Commerce and Industry. The forum attracted 520 attendees, including senior officials, business leaders and company representatives.

The trade relationship has continued to expand. According to Dubai Media Office, non-oil trade between Dubai and South Africa reached AED 29.2 billion in 2025, representing growth of 265% over the past decade from AED 8 billion in 2016. Dubai Chamber of Commerce also reported 873 active South African member companies by the end of Q1 2026, including 45 new companies that joined during the quarter.

For investors and business observers, the mission highlights Dubai's role as a platform for cross-regional trade between the Middle East and Africa. It also points to continued demand for sector-specific matchmaking, business councils and trade corridors that can support private-sector expansion beyond domestic markets.

News Watch: Dubai's non-oil trade agenda remains closely tied to international business connectivity, with African markets continuing to feature in long-term expansion strategies for manufacturing, logistics, retail, healthcare, technology and investment services.
