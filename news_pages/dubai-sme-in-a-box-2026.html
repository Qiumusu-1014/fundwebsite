Dubai Launches SME in a Box to Streamline Business Setup for Entrepreneurs

Dubai's Department of Economy and Tourism has launched SME in a Box, a platform bringing licensing, banking, payments, logistics and telecom services into one onboarding journey for founders.

Dubai SME SME in a Box entrepreneurship business setup D33 banking payments logistics Dubai Founders HQ Invest in Dubai

DUBAI, UAE - Dubai's Department of Economy and Tourism announced on June 4, 2026, the launch of SME in a Box, a platform designed to help entrepreneurs start and operate small and medium-sized businesses in Dubai with fewer separate onboarding steps.

The platform gives founders a single entry point for essential operational services, including licensing support, banking, digital payments, logistics, telecommunications and related business services. Instead of approaching multiple providers independently, companies can access a curated partner network through one coordinated journey.

According to the Government of Dubai Media Office, partner offers under SME in a Box are estimated to unlock more than AED 80,000 in potential value per business, depending on which services an SME activates. The initiative is also expected to save founders up to 200 hours that would otherwise be spent comparing providers, negotiating commercial terms and completing separate onboarding processes.

The launch includes 18 private-sector partners across banking, payments, telecommunications, logistics and business services. Named partners include Emirates NBD, Network International, Commercial Bank of Dubai, du, Paymob, Ziina, Qashio, Bayzat, Mamo, Aramex, Tabby, DHL and Abu Dhabi Islamic Bank, among others.

Digital-first services such as payments, logistics and telecommunications may be activated within as little as 24 hours through integrated onboarding and automated eligibility checks. Services requiring standard regulatory review, including corporate banking and licensing approval, continue to follow existing requirements while benefiting from coordinated pre-validation.

For investors and business observers, the initiative signals Dubai's continued focus on business formation, SME enablement and digital operating infrastructure under the Dubai Economic Agenda D33. Future phases are expected to add deeper digital integration, connect the platform with Invest in Dubai and integrate it within Dubai Founders HQ to support companies from setup through scale-up.

News Watch: SME support platforms are becoming an increasingly important part of Dubai's investment proposition, particularly for founders seeking faster access to financial services, payment tools, logistics partners and market-entry support.
