UAE Ranks First Globally in Entrepreneurship Ecosystem for Fifth Consecutive Year

ABU DHABI, UAE - The Global Entrepreneurship Monitor 2025/2026 report ranked the UAE first globally for the fifth consecutive year, recognizing it as one of the world's best economies for starting and operating new businesses.

Citing the report, the Emirates News Agency said the UAE ranked first among high-income economies across eight indicators, including infrastructure, government policy support, tax and administrative efficiency, government entrepreneurship programs, research and development transfer, market-entry dynamics, ease of entry under regulatory burdens and entrepreneurial education.

The report also placed the UAE second globally in entrepreneurial finance and financing accessibility. More than one fifth of adults in the UAE are involved in starting new businesses, with total early-stage entrepreneurial activity reaching 19.2%.

The report said entrepreneurial activity remains strong among both UAE nationals and residents, reflecting the country's ability to attract international talent, cultivate domestic entrepreneurs and support women-led ventures.

Abdulla bin Touq Al Marri, Minister of Economy and Tourism, said the ranking reflects the UAE's policy achievements in innovation, the knowledge economy, small and medium-sized enterprises and high-potential industries. These priorities align with the We the UAE 2031 vision, particularly in artificial intelligence, renewable energy, space and fintech.

News Watch: The ranking strengthens the UAE's appeal to global capital, innovative companies and technology talent while providing measurable support for its economic diversification strategy.

Sources: WAM, Global Entrepreneurship Monitor
