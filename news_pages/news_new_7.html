UAE Central Bank Launches "Digital Dirham" for Cross-Border Trade

ABU DHABI, UAE — The Central Bank of the UAE has officially launched its CBDC (Central Bank Digital Currency) strategy, marking a historic milestone in the digitization of the region's economy.

The "Digital Dirham" is designed to facilitate faster, cheaper, and more secure cross-border payments. UAE Royal Capital Funding has been selected as one of the key private sector partners to pilot the first large-scale blockchain settlement using the new currency.

"This technology will revolutionize how we manage sovereign assets and international investments," commented the Fund's Chief Technology Officer. The pilot program will involve settling trade transactions with key partners in India and China, bypassing traditional SWIFT delays.
